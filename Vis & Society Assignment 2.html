Subtheme: Exploring speculative investment (“flipped properties”)

Dataset

    This dataset presents extensive statistics on residential sales within the City of Boston spanning from 2000 to 2023. It provides detailed information on recorded sales transactions, including seller and buyer data, sale prices, property locations, and the classification of sales as "flips." The dataset is sourced from The Warren Group. As someone with a background in interior architecture, I was intrigued by the concept of fix-and-flip housing and sought to understand the nuances of how these properties are transformed. This dataset offers the opportunity to explore these differences and gain fresh insights into flipped properties.

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. What is the spatial distribution pattern of flipped properties across different neighborhoods in Boston?
  2. Did COVID-19 impact transactions in Boston, and what trends emerged during this period?
  3. Who comprises the buyer/seller types for flipped properties, and to what extent do their purchases contribute to the escalation of rental and housing prices (based on data)?

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

Overall, this comprehensive data analysis of residential sales in Boston from 2000 to 2023, sourced from The Warren Group, delves into the spatial distribution of flipped properties, the impact of COVID-19 on transactions, and the dynamics of buyer/seller types. Despite flips constituting a small portion of transactions, they exhibit an even distribution across neighborhoods and a notable increase in average prices, particularly in the 2020s. While investors predominantly target higher-priced properties like condos, non-investors focus on more affordable options, suggesting a possible parallel market. However, it's challenging to conclude that professional investors haven't influenced the market dynamics against mainstream viewpoints. Given that condos often consist of multiple units, their involvement in flips could contribute to evictions or rent increases. Further exploration is necessary to fully comprehend these implications.
